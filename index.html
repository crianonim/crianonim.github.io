I've been working all my life in service industry making sandwiches or coffee during the day and then playing with programming after. I think my personal interests below will show you my characteristics that lead me to your bootcamp.

LanguagesBotanyCookingHistoryTechnology
Since childhood I've loved learning languages. In London I just kept going thanks to a delicious stream of cultures and languages that piqued my curiosity even more. I'm fluent in Portugues do Brasil, can have una buena conversación en español, order food in un ristorante italiano. I like to think that my JavaScript is becoming more fluent as well...
Nothing bring me more joy then watching something grow! Especially if it's something i planted, sowed, started from scratch. Then with careful cultivation it develops through different stages into a new, living thing.
I like learning new recipes (algorithms?) form others and improve my craft. I like as well to experiment and come up with new. The best is that you share the fruit of your labour and form a little community even if ephemeral and limited in space to your dining table. Oh, and I like to eat!
Magna carta, the Tudor court, colonialism? I love to learn about it all. Studying history let's us learn from exprience of others in the past. People had so many great ideas we can copy, and have done horrible mistakes we should avoid. It's often exciting narrative as well.
Technology lets me transcend my biology. I can communicate with others without space limitations. I am able to interact with my internal creativity, be equal to ancient gods with my powers to make things as I see fit with code. I relax playing video games (there are games that let me farm, cook, tell a story using a forein language, tick all my hobbies). Technology is fun!
